# Mixed Verb Tenses Exercises – ANSWERS

## Present Simple / Present Continuous

1. You **are going** to school now.
2. **Does he eat** rice every day?
3. I **am working** at the moment.
4. **Does she come** to London often?
5. He **is playing** tennis now.
6. **Is she cooking** fish at the moment?
7. They **don't study** at nights.
8. He **isn't reading** a magazine today.
9. **Are you watching** a film now?
10. They **go** to a restaurant every Saturday.
11. She **doesn't go** to the cinema every weekend.
12. You usually **arrive** late.

## Past Simple / Present Perfect

1. Last night I **lost** my keys.
2. I **have lost** my keys – can you help me look for them?
3. I **have visited** Paris three times.
4. Last year I **visited** Paris.
5. I **knew** my grandmother for a few years.
6. I **have known** Julie for three years.
7. Sorry, I **have missed** the bus.
8. I **missed** the bus, and then I **missed** the plane too.
9. Last month I **went** to Scotland.
10. John isn't here now. He **has gone** to the shops.
11. I **have drunk** three cups of coffee this morning.
12. I **drank** five cups of coffee yesterday.

## Past Simple / Past Continuous

1. Susie **was watching** a film when she **heard** the noise.
2. What **were you doing** when I **called** you last night?
3. Yesterday I **went** to the library, next I **had** a swim, later I **met** my friends for coffee.
4. We **were playing** tennis when John **hurt** his ankle.
5. What **were they doing** at 10 pm last night?
6. He **was taking** a shower when the telephone **rang**.
7. He **was** in the shower when the postman **arrived**.
8. When I **walked** into the room, everyone **was working**.
9. He **was living** in Russia when the Revolution **started**.
10. When her train **got** to the station, we **were waiting** on the platform.

## Past Simple / Past Perfect

1. We had already eaten when Paul **came** home.
2. Last year Peter **passed** all his exams.
3. When I **got** to the airport, I discovered I had forgotten my passport.
4. I went to the library, then I **bought** some milk and went home.
5. I opened my bag to find that I **had forgotten** my credit card.
6. Julie was very pleased to see that John **had cleaned** the kitchen.
7. It **hadn't rained** all summer, so the grass was completely dead.
8. John **played** the piano when he was a child, but he doesn't play now.
9. After arriving home, I realized I **hadn't bought** any milk.
10. First I tidied the flat, then I **sat** down and had a cup of tea.
