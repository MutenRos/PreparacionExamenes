# Modal Exercises – ANSWERS

## 1. Complete with can / can't / could / couldn't

a. I'm afraid I **can't** come to your party next week.
b. He **could** run 100 metres in 11 seconds.
c. I **couldn't** eat anything.
d. I **can't** hear you very well.
e. **Can** you please help me wash the car?
f. Jessica **can** speak French and also Italian.
g. She **can't** finish painting the room by tomorrow.
h. We **can't** sleep. You are making too much noise.
i. In my country you **can't** get married when you are 16.
j. Last night I **couldn't** get into my house.

## 2. Complete with must / mustn't / have to / don't have to / should / shouldn't

a. Pupils **don't have to** attend the game. *(optional)*
b. You **mustn't** leave knives in places where babies can reach them.
c. The doctor said I **shouldn't** eat too many sweets and potatoes.
d. You **mustn't** play with the knife!
e. I **have to** pay the telephone bill next week.
f. People **mustn't** smoke in non-smoking areas.
g. Students **don't have to** go to school on Sundays.
h. All children under 16 **must** / **have to** go to school. *(law)*
i. I really think you **should** cut your hair.
j. I like Sundays because I **don't have to** get up early.

## 3. Should / shouldn't sentences

a. Liz **should go away** for a few days.
b. I **should look for another job**.
c. Jack **shouldn't go to bed so late**.
d. You **should take a photograph**.
e. Sue **shouldn't use her car so much**.
f. Bill **should put some pictures on the walls**.

## 4. Choose the right modal verb

a. The doctor says I **should** rest more.
b. When I was young, I **had to** get up very early.
c. **Could** you ride a bike when you were three years old?
d. You **mustn't** smoke and you **should** do exercise.
e. I **couldn't** speak English when I was nine.
f. **Can** you help me? **Can** I use your phone?
g. Children under sixteen **mustn't** drive cars.

## 5. Correct the mistakes

a. He must ~~to~~ be there by six o'clock. → **He must be there by six o'clock.**
b. You ~~don't have to~~ park here. → **You mustn't park here.** *(It's forbidden)*
c. You ~~should talked~~ to him. → **You should talk to him.**
d. He ~~cans~~ speak three languages. → **He can speak three languages.**
e. You ~~have~~ see the doctor. → **You have to see the doctor.**
f. ~~Did you can~~ finish your work? → **Could you finish your work last night?**
